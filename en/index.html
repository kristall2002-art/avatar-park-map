Zhangjiajie travel tips

🛂

Visa-free entry to China

Many nationalities can enter China visa-free for up to 30 days (including Russia, EU, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Korea and more). Check the latest list for your country.

💳

Payments in Zhangjiajie

China runs on WeChat Pay and Alipay. Foreign cards are accepted at hotels. Carry some yuan cash for small purchases inside the park.

📱

Internet and VPN in China

Google, Instagram, WhatsApp — all blocked. Install a VPN before your trip. Google Maps doesn't work either — our map replaces it inside the park.

🚆

Getting to Zhangjiajie

Hehua Airport (DYG) — flights from Beijing, Shanghai. High-speed trains (高铁) to Zhangjiajie West station. Tickets on trip.com.

🌡

Best time for Zhangjiajie

March–April and September–November. Summer is hot and crowded. Winter has few visitors and sometimes snow on the Avatar Mountains peaks.

🐒

Monkeys in the park

Zhangjiajie park is home to monkeys — they steal food and belongings. Keep your backpack closed and don't carry bags in your hand.

👟

What to pack for Zhangjiajie

Comfortable shoes are a must — up to 25,000 steps a day in the mountains. A rain jacket is useful. Bring snacks — food inside the park is expensive.

🎫

Glass Bridge — online only

Glass Bridge tickets are sold ONLINE ONLY — you cannot buy them on the spot. Book in advance, your original passport is required.

Do I need a visa to visit Zhangjiajie?

Many nationalities can enter China visa-free for up to 30 days (Russia, most EU countries, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Korea and more). Check the current list for your country — the policy expanded significantly in 2024–2025.

How much is the entry ticket to Zhangjiajie National Park?

The combined ticket costs ¥239 (about $33) and is valid for 4 days. The Glass Bridge is paid separately — ¥256, sold online only.

How many days do I need for Zhangjiajie?

Ideally 4 days — exactly the validity of the entry ticket. Day 1: Avatar Mountains and Yuanjiajie. Day 2: Tianzishan and the cave. Day 3: Glass Bridge and Baofeng Lake. Day 4: Yangjiajie.

How do I get to Zhangjiajie?

The nearest airport is Zhangjiajie Hehua (DYG). No direct flights from Europe or America — connect via Beijing, Shanghai or Guangzhou. From the airport to Wulingyuan it is about 1 hour by taxi. You can also take a high-speed train.

Why do I need a map if I have Google Maps?

Google Maps is blocked in China and does not work without a VPN. Our map is hand-drawn with detailed trails, elevators, cable cars and timings. It works fully offline.

What are the Avatar Mountains and where are they?

The Avatar Mountains is the popular name for the sandstone pillars of Zhangjiajie National Park, Hunan Province, China. One of the pillars was officially renamed Avatar Hallelujah Mountain. The park is part of the Wulingyuan scenic area — a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

When is the best time to visit Zhangjiajie?

The best time is March–April and September–November. Few tourists, comfortable temperatures of 10–20°C. Summer is hot and crowded. Winter brings snow on the peaks — stunningly beautiful but cold.

Can I visit Zhangjiajie independently?

Yes, the park is excellent for independent travel. Free eco-buses run inside and signs are clear. Our map helps you plan a route. Names in English and Chinese make it easy to communicate with drivers and staff.

Up-to-date Zhangjiajie National Park map

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园) is one of the most striking natural sites in China and the world. Located in Hunan Province, the park became globally famous after James Cameron's Avatar — its sandstone pillars inspired the floating mountains of Pandora. Today Zhangjiajie welcomes millions of tourists from every country every year.

Avatar Mountains — what to see in 4 days

The entry ticket to Zhangjiajie National Park costs ¥239 and is valid for 4 days — enough to see all the main attractions. Spend day one on the Yuanjiajie plateau with Avatar Hallelujah Mountain and ride the Bailong Elevator, the tallest outdoor elevator in the world (326 m). Dedicate day two to Tianzishan Mountain with the Ten-Mile Gallery and the Yellow Dragon Cave. On day three visit the Glass Bridge over the Grand Canyon (430 m, tickets online only) and the picturesque Baofeng Lake. Day four is for Yangjiajie or the Huangshi Zhai panorama platform.
